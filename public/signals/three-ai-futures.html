Executive Summary

This analysis examines three divergent scenarios for the AI industry and maps their implications onto specific companies, sectors, and career paths. Each scenario is assessed for probability, magnitude of impact, and leading indicators that would signal we are heading in that direction.

The scenarios are not mutually exclusive. Elements of all three could materialize simultaneously, and the most likely actual future is some weighted combination. The analysis is designed to be actionable for an investor holding TotalEnergies (TTE) stock and considering career transitions from oil and gas into technology-adjacent roles.

Current Market Context (June 2026)

Before diving into scenarios, the current state of play matters. The AI infrastructure build-out is the largest capital investment cycle since the internet era, arguably in history.

AI Infrastructure Spending

Hyperscalers (Amazon, Microsoft, Google, Meta) have committed to $725 billion in AI capex for 2026, up from $610B estimated in February. Goldman Sachs models $765B. J.P. Morgan projects $5 trillion cumulative through 2030. As a share of US GDP, this exceeds the Apollo program, the interstate highway system, and every major US capital project except the Louisiana Purchase.

GPU Market

Nvidia posted $62.3 billion in data center revenue in fiscal Q4 2026, up 75% YoY. GPU rental prices (H100) are up 20% in 2026. Lead times for data center GPUs run 36–52 weeks. The bottleneck is TSMC’s CoWoS (Chip-on-Wafer-on-Substrate) advanced packaging capacity and HBM (High-Bandwidth Memory) supply from SK Hynix, Samsung, and Micron.

Open-Source AI

The performance gap between open-source and proprietary models has effectively collapsed. The MMLU (Massive Multitask Language Understanding) benchmark gap narrowed from 17.5 to 0.3 percentage points in one year. Open-weight models now lag state-of-the-art by approximately 3 months. DeepSeek V3 matched GPT-4o on most benchmarks at $5.5M training cost vs. OpenAI’s estimated hundreds of millions.

AI Lab Economics

Anthropic hit $47B annualized revenue (May 2026), surpassing OpenAI at ~$33B. Anthropic filed confidentially for IPO at $965B valuation. OpenAI is running negative 122% operating margins with projected $25B in annual infrastructure costs. The divergence: Anthropic is 80% enterprise; OpenAI is more consumer-heavy.

TotalEnergies Position

TTE signed a 1 GW solar PPA with Google (Feb 2026) for Texas data centers—its largest US renewable deal, delivering 28 TWh over 15 years. TTE is deploying a 10 GW US portfolio (solar, wind, battery). The company is positioning as an energy management partner for hyperscaler data centers, not just a commodity power seller.

Scenario 1: Heavily Compute-Constrained World

Premise: Tokens are precious. GPU rents reach 10–100X current levels. Demand for AI compute grows faster than supply can expand, creating sustained scarcity.

Probability: 20–30% for sustained extreme scarcity (5-year horizon)

This is the least likely scenario in extreme form because supply is responding aggressively: TSMC is expanding CoWoS capacity, Samsung/Micron are bringing HBM online in late 2026–2027, and Nvidia’s Rubin platform promises 10X inference cost reduction vs. Blackwell. However, a moderate version (persistent GPU tightness, 2–5X premium) is already partially in play and could persist 2–3 years.

Scenario 2: Model Weights Don’t Matter, Only Compute Does

Premise: Open-source catches up completely. Proprietary model advantages evaporate. The moat shifts entirely to compute access, distribution, and integration.

Probability: 35–45% (5-year horizon)

This is already substantially underway. The MMLU gap is 0.3 points. The frontier lag is ~3 months. DeepSeek proved you can train frontier models at 1/50th the cost. The question is whether proprietary labs can maintain ANY durable advantage in model quality, or whether compute access and ecosystem become the only moats.

Key Nuance: Anthropic and OpenAI Aren’t Dead

Even in this scenario, pure model labs have several pivots available. Anthropic’s enterprise relationships (80% of $47B ARR) create switching costs independent of model quality. OpenAI’s consumer brand (900M users, 5% paying) has distribution value. Both could pivot toward:

  • Compute-as-a-service (use their massive GPU reservations to resell)
  • Platform/ecosystem plays (API tooling, fine-tuning infrastructure)
  • Safety/alignment services (regulatory moat if AI safety legislation passes)
  • Enterprise integration (become the “AI department” for Fortune 500s)

Scenario 3: All Mundane Office Work Automated

Premise: White-collar productivity explodes. AI handles email, scheduling, report generation, data analysis, compliance, and routine decision-making. A 5-person company operates at the capacity of a 50-person company.

Probability: 40–60% for partial realization within 5 years

This is the highest probability scenario in partial form. Microsoft’s AI CEO publicly stated (Feb 2026) that “most office jobs will be automated” within 18 months. McKinsey estimates 375 million workers globally may need to switch careers by 2030. AI-augmented high-skill workers are projected to earn ~71 percentage points more than middle-skill workers in disrupted roles.

The Bubble Question

With $725B in 2026 capex and projections of $1T+ by 2027, the revenue-spending mismatch is the elephant in the room. Hyperscaler AI capex as a share of US GDP exceeds the Apollo program. Stanford and UC Berkeley economists question whether returns justify the scale. The base case for justifying this spend requires Scenario 3 (massive productivity automation) to materialize substantially.

The risk for investors: if Scenario 3 underdelivers, the infrastructure built for Scenarios 1–2 becomes overcapacity, and winners from those scenarios (especially data center REITs and power companies) face a correction. This is the bear case for TTE’s data center power strategy.

Career Transition Implications

Your background in petroleum engineering + project management + emerging coding skills positions you well for several high-value transition paths:

1. AI/Energy Infrastructure Program Management

The data center build-out is fundamentally an energy and infrastructure project. Companies like TotalEnergies, Constellation Energy, NextEra, and the hyperscalers themselves need people who understand large-scale project management, energy systems, and can work at the intersection of technical and commercial. This leverages your PM experience and oil/gas engineering background directly.

2. Technical Product/Solutions Architecture

Your growing coding skills + domain expertise make you a candidate for roles translating business requirements into technical solutions. GCP certifications (which you’re already considering) are directly relevant—GCP is the fastest-growing cloud platform (63% YoY) and is disproportionately winning AI workloads.

3. AI-Augmented Consulting (Energy/Industrial)

In Scenario 3, routine consulting gets automated but domain-expert consulting becomes MORE valuable. Someone who understands EOR, reservoir engineering, and energy systems AND can use AI tools effectively commands a premium. This path offers geographic flexibility.

4. Remote-First Technical Roles

The >80% remote requirement narrows the field but aligns with the trend. Cloud platform roles, DevOps/MLOps (Machine Learning Operations), and technical program management at tech companies routinely offer full remote. The GCP certification path directly feeds into this.

Key Timing Insight: The AI-augmented worker wage premium (71pp vs. disrupted peers) suggests the highest ROI career move is becoming proficient with AI tools NOW, not waiting. Your current coding/AI learning trajectory is the right bet.
